Overview

Experiences of suburban landscapes are plentiful, diverse, and multi-faceted. Factors such as wealth, geo-location, planned versus created spaces, and racial composition result in neighbourhoods which differ significantly eachother. Suburbias fragmentation enables distinct sensorial experiences and individualised worlds for different members of Suburbia. Through the deployment of graphic techniques, I propose a series of representations of these themes. This installation will act as a minimised representation of my local area Maroubra. Through this, I aim to represent the tension between repetition and heterogeneity within the suburban landscape. This exhibition is based on the notion of personal stories. It aims to gathers and represents a wide variety of different stories. Some short, long, strange, touching, heartbreaking, or funny. After which, these stories are collated and recontextualised in the forms of animations, sculptures, and graphic interventions. Audiences are invited to view the world through lens of others in their neighbourhood, and to develop empathy and understanding of the other. These personal stories are placed on the backdrop of monotonous graphic imagery and colours derived from colours and shapes of the local area and the minimalist design more broadly.
